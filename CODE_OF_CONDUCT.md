# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors and maintainers of matrix-lib, pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior
- **Be excellent to each other** — technical disagreements are welcome, personal attacks are not
- **Question everything** — including our own assumptions and findings
- **Share knowledge generously** — we're all here to learn from each other
- **Measure before you believe** — and share your measurements
- **Help newcomers** — explain concepts clearly and patiently
- **Celebrate discoveries** — whether they're yours or someone else's

### Unacceptable Behavior
- **Personal attacks** or derogatory comments
- **Dismissive attitudes** toward questions or contributions
- **Unsubstantiated claims** without data or evidence
- **Gatekeeping** — "this is too advanced for you" attitudes
- **Spam or off-topic content** in technical discussions

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:
- GitHub repositories and issues
- Pull request discussions
- Community discussions and forums
- Any other spaces representing the project

## Enforcement

### How to Report
If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:
1. **Contact the maintainers** via GitHub issue or email
2. **Provide details** — what happened, when, and who was involved
3. **We'll investigate** promptly and confidentially

### Consequences
Violations will result in:
- **Warning** for first offenses
- **Temporary ban** for repeated violations
- **Permanent ban** for severe or repeated violations

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/), version 2.1, and inspired by the principles of scientific discourse and technical excellence.

## Our Community Values

### Technical Excellence
We value rigorous, evidence-based discussion. Claims should be backed by measurements, not opinions.

### Curiosity and Learning
Everyone starts somewhere. Questions are celebrated, not mocked. Teaching others is as valuable as writing code.

### Collaboration Over Competition
We're not competing against each other. We're competing against ignorance and suboptimal performance.

### Inclusivity Through Merit
We welcome contributors from all backgrounds who demonstrate curiosity, rigor, and the willingness to learn.
